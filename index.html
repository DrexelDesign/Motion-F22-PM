10 Princples of Motion

Based on the 12 principles of Animation by Ollie Johnson & Frank Thomas.

1

Timing & Spacing

This refers to the speed of actions. It is essential for establishing mood and realism. More frames create slower action and fewer frames creates faster action.

2

Easing

This refers to the speed of actions. It is essential for establishing mood and realism. Natural movement in which a change in direction decelerates into the change and accelerates out.

3

Mass & Weight

An animator must give a character a presence in its environment through proper portrayal of their mass and weight. In most cases these should remain the same.

4

Anticipation

Presenting short actions or hints to a viewer of what is about to happen. Prepares the audience for what is going to happen next.

5

Arcs

Most things move in an arc of some kind. Motion in animations and humans occurs along curved paths.

6

Squash & Stretch

The deformation of objects in reaction to the laws of physics. Provides the illusion that an object has weight and volume.

7

Follow Through

This is the way in which momentum acts on a moving object to cause extra motion even after the initial action has stopped.

8

Exaggeration

Perfect imitations of the real world can appear dull and static. Often it is necessary to make things bigger, faster and brighter to present them in an acceptable manner.

9

Secondary Action

These animations support the action of the principle animation. They provide the scene with a stronger sense of realism.
